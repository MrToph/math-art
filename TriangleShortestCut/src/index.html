Solution

Notice, how a hexagon are just six equilateral triangles (start the animation). We can reduce the above problem to finding the shape of minimum perimeter that splits a hexagon into two parts of equal area. For a given area, the shape with minimum circumference is always the circle, so in the original problem the arc is the shortest cut for the triangle.
